Why You Should Like the Cold

Choosing the cold on purpose is practice for doing hard things when they actually matter.

Jump to Reasons See Streak Data

Reasons to Embrace the Cold

  1. Physical benefits
    • Improved alertness and energy.
    • Practicing controlled breathing under stress.
    • Faster post‑training recovery sensations.
  2. Mental benefits
    • Proof you can do something uncomfortable on purpose.
    • Clear signal to start the day with a win.
    • Better separation between “feel like it” and “do it anyway”.
  3. Life carryover
    • Helps with studying when tired.
    • Makes early workouts or long projects feel normal.
    • Builds an identity of someone who does hard things.

Cold Plunge Basics

How often?

Many people aim for several short plunges per week, stacking them next to habits like workouts or morning routines.

How long?

Time depends on temperature and experience—start short and focus on calm breathing instead of chasing hero numbers.

Mindset

The goal is not comfort. The goal is to notice discomfort, relax your face and breathing, and choose to stay present anyway.
